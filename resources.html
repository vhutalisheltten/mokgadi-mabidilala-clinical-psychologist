Relevant Suggestions

Simple guidance before you book.

Use these prompts to decide what kind of support to ask for. They do not replace professional assessment or emergency care.

When to Reach Out

Consider booking when emotions, thoughts, stress, grief, trauma, relationship concerns, or adjustment issues start affecting sleep, work, school, relationships, or daily functioning.

What to Share When Booking

Your name, contact number, preferred consultation format, whether this is self-referral or referral by another professional, and a brief description of what support is needed.

Referral Options

The practice accepts self-referrals, healthcare practitioner referrals, and organisational referrals. If another provider referred you, bring any relevant referral note or background information.

Before a First Session

Think about what has changed, what feels most difficult, what you have already tried, and what you hope will be different after support.

Crisis Support

If there is immediate danger, use emergency support first.

If someone may harm themselves or someone else, or there is a medical emergency, contact emergency services or go to the nearest emergency department.

Call 112 from a mobile phone Call 10111 for police emergency Call 10177 for ambulance or fire SADAG Suicide Crisis Line: 0800 567 567
